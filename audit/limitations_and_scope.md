# Limitations and Scope

## Sample boundary
- Main estimation window is constrained by aligned quarterly observations with valid expectation mapping and macro controls.
- Inference is made within the observed sample period only and is not presented as a structural invariant outside this window.

## Identification boundary
- The main design uses national salience-IV because province-level exposure required for staggered DiD is unavailable in current data.
- Exclusion is argued and stress-tested but cannot be proven in aggregate time-series settings.
- Weak-IV diagnostics indicate low first-stage strength in the current run (NW F = 1.164; wide AR interval), so causal language must remain restrained.

## Measurement boundary
- Carlson-Parkin quantification depends on threshold assumptions and response-share quality.
- Uncertain responses are modeled through bound analysis, yet the true latent mapping remains unobserved.

## Mechanism boundary
- Competing mechanism tests use aggregate proxies for information rigidity; micro-level expectation panels would sharpen discrimination.
- Dynamic evidence from LP and appendix BVAR is informative but still sample-limited under short quarterly histories.

## Policy mapping boundary
- Backtest gains are reported in reduced-form forecast metrics (RMSE, MAE, coverage).
- Operational use requires real-time data revisions, communication timing, and institutional constraints not fully modeled here.

## External validity
- Results are designed for China-specific institutional and media environments and should not be extrapolated mechanically to other economies.
- Future work should combine province/household micro data, richer media salience measures, and alternative instruments to tighten causal interpretation.
