\section{Heterogeneity}

I evaluate heterogeneity along uncertainty regimes and information-friction intensity because diagnostic behavior should strengthen when salient signals dominate limited attention. Table~\ref{tab:heterogeneity_interactions} estimates interaction models where the revision coefficient is allowed to vary with high-EPU states, high-GPR states, and low effective survey-response shares. The interaction signs and implied high-state coefficients indicate whether overreaction intensifies exactly where attention constraints should bind more tightly.

A monotonicity check complements binary interactions by splitting salience into terciles. Table~\ref{tab:heterogeneity_monotonicity} and Figure~\ref{fig:heterogeneity_salience} show the revision coefficient across low, medium, and high salience groups with confidence intervals. This design does not claim micro-level household heterogeneity, which the current data do not identify, but it tests whether aggregate state dependence follows a monotone pattern consistent with mechanism predictions rather than arbitrary subgroup instability.

The heterogeneity evidence feeds directly into policy interpretation because communication strategies are state contingent by construction. If diagnostic intensity rises when uncertainty is high or response quality deteriorates, policymakers face asymmetric risks from aggressively interpreted signals in exactly those periods when they most rely on communication tools, which pushes the analysis from static average effects toward operational regime management.
