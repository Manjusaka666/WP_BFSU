\section{Mechanism Competition}

The central mechanism comparison is between diagnostic overreaction and information rigidity in the Coibion--Gorodnichenko tradition. Diagnostic expectations imply that stronger upward revisions predict more negative subsequent forecast errors, while rigidity implies sluggish updating and positive persistence from lagged errors. I estimate both channels in separate and joint specifications so that one mechanism does not win by construction through omitted regressors.

Table~\ref{tab:mechanism_horse_race} reports the horse-race estimates. The revision coefficient remains the dominant term with the diagnostic sign in joint models, while the lagged-error channel weakens once revision information enters. The same table reports a state-dependent interaction with high uncertainty; this interaction tests whether diagnostic behavior is amplified when salient narratives are more likely to displace baseline beliefs.

Table~\ref{tab:revision_predicts_error} and Figure~\ref{fig:mechanism_chain} show the core diagnostic chain in levels: bins with larger positive revisions map into more negative subsequent forecast errors. This pattern is difficult to reconcile with pure sticky-information underreaction without adding an auxiliary overreaction channel, so the data favor a diagnostic interpretation in the sample where measurement and identification remain valid.
